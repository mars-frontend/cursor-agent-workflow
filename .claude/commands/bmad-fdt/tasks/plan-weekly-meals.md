# /plan-weekly-meals Task

When this command is used, execute the following task:

# Plan Weekly Meals Task

Create a balanced, personalized meal plan for the upcoming week that aligns with your nutritional goals and lifestyle.

## Meal Planning Process

### Goal & Constraint Definition

[[LLM: Understand the user's nutritional targets, dietary preferences, cooking constraints, and budget considerations.]]

1. **Nutritional Targets**
   - Daily calorie goal
   - Macronutrient ratios
   - Micronutrient priorities
   - Health objectives

2. **Constraints & Preferences**
   - Dietary restrictions (allergies, intolerances)
   - Disliked foods
   - Cooking skill level
   - Available cooking time
   - Budget limitations
   - Cuisine preferences

3. **Lifestyle Factors**
   - Eating out frequency
   - Social meal plans
   - Work schedule
   - Exercise routine
   - Sleep schedule

### Meal Selection

[[LLM: Generate breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snack options that meet nutritional targets.]]

1. **Breakfast Options** (3-4 choices)
   - High-protein options
   - Quick options for busy days
   - Varied carbohydrate sources
   - Micronutrient diversity

2. **Lunch Ideas** (3-4 choices)
   - Work-friendly options
   - Mix of home and restaurant
   - Protein and veggie balanced
   - Batch-cookable options

3. **Dinner Selections** (5-6 choices)
   - Varied cuisines
   - Different protein sources
   - Seasonal ingredients
   - Prep-ahead options
   - Quick options

4. **Snack Recommendations**
   - Portable options
   - Desk-friendly snacks
   - Pre-workout options
   - Evening options

### Weekly Calendar Assembly

[[LLM: Arrange selected meals into a calendar, ensuring nutritional balance and variety.]]

1. **Layout Days**
   - Monday through Sunday
   - Track special events or dining out
   - Note any constraints for specific days

2. **Assign Meals**
   - Rotate main dishes
   - Space similar foods 2-3 days apart
   - Plan leftovers strategically
   - Balance light and hearty meals

3. **Nutritional Verification**
   - Check each day hits targets
   - Confirm macro balance
   - Verify micronutrient diversity
   - Assess hydration opportunities

### Shopping List Generation

[[LLM: Create organized shopping list with quantities needed for the week's plan.]]

1. **By Category**
   - Proteins (meat, fish, eggs, legumes)
   - Vegetables (fresh and frozen)
   - Fruits
   - Grains and bread
   - Dairy
   - Pantry staples

2. **Quantity Calculations**
   - Based on servings needed
   - Include batch cooking amounts
   - Account for spoilage
   - Note shelf-life items

3. **Budget Optimization**
   - Generic vs. brand options
   - Seasonal pricing
   - Bulk buying opportunities
   - Cost per meal tracking

## Weekly Plan Deliverable

### Calendar Format
```
MONDAY
- Breakfast: [Meal] - [Calories] - [Macros]
- Lunch: [Meal] - [Calories] - [Macros]
- Dinner: [Meal] - [Calories] - [Macros]
- Snacks: [Item] - [Calories] - [Macros]
Daily Total: [Calories] [Macros]

[Repeat for Tue-Sun]
```

### Recipe Cards
For each meal include:
- Ingredients with quantities
- Preparation steps
- Cooking time
- Macro breakdown
- Storage instructions

### Shopping List
Organized by store section with quantities

### Prep Notes
- What can be prepped ahead
- Storage recommendations
- Reheating instructions
- Shelf-life tracking

## Flexibility & Adjustments

- Include 2-3 "swap" options for each meal slot
- Note compatible ingredient substitutions
- Plan for last-minute restaurant options
- Build in buffer days for spontaneity

## Implementation Tips

1. **Prep Day** (typically Sunday)
   - Wash and chop vegetables
   - Cook batch proteins
   - Prepare base grains
   - Portion snacks

2. **Daily Prep**
   - 15-30 min morning prep
   - Use pre-prepped ingredients
   - Combine with previous day leftovers

3. **Batch Cooking**
   - Cook double portions for leftovers
   - Freeze portions for later
   - Mix and match components

## Success Metrics

- All 7 days planned with specific meals
- Shopping list complete before store visit
- 90%+ adherence to plan (allow flexibility)
- Nutritional targets met within 10%
- Minimal food waste
